Seemandhra and Telangana regions are sitting on potential oil and natural gas reserves, according to a report by Director General of Hydrocarbons (DGH). As a matter of fact, the Krishna Godavari Basin (KG Basin) in the east coast of Andhra Pradesh has already been categorised by DGH as a region with proven commercial productivity of hydrocarbons and Oil and gas ltd. has been in the thick of extraction activity in over 50,000 sq. km area. The Pranahita-Godavari stretch in Adilabad district in the Telangana region and Krishna-Godavari stretch in Kadapa district in Rayalaseema region have been identified as potentially prospective categories by the DGH. “These regions can be lined up for bidding by New Exploration Licensing Policy (NELP). They are most likely to be focused in the coming days,” B. Sam Bob, the present principal secretary to Municipal Administration and Urban Development, who served as Union Secretary of Oil Industry Development Board, Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas earlier, said. As per a report by DGH, about 15,000 square kilometres area in Adilabad district is potential region for exploration of oil and natural gas. Over 39,000 sq. km. stretch in Kadapa district has been earmarked by DGH as a potential exploration site for the rare natural resource. There have been significant developments in the exploration of oil and natural gas over the last twelve years in exploring the hydrocarbon potential in the country. As of now, the unexplored area in India, which was about 50 per cent in 1995-96, has come down to 15 per cent, according to DGH.
